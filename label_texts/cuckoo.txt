Cuckoos (Genus: Cuculus) intrigue birdwatchers with their unique reproductive strategy and distinctive calls. Found worldwide in various habitats, from forests to grasslands, these migratory birds practice brood parasitism, laying eggs in other bird species' nests. Their striking appearances and elusive nature captivate enthusiasts, often identified by their characteristic calls. Despite their sneaky tactics, cuckoos play vital roles as insect predators and contributors to biodiversity. Observing cuckoos provides insights into avian reproduction and the intricate relationships within ecosystems.
