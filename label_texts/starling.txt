Starlings (Genus: Sturnus) captivate birdwatchers with their iridescent plumage and dynamic behaviors. These medium-sized passerines are widespread, inhabiting diverse habitats. Known for their melodious songs and mesmerizing flocking displays called murmurations, starlings showcase remarkable social and vocal abilities. Despite being considered pests in some regions, they play vital ecological roles as seed dispersers and insect predators. Observing starlings offers birdwatchers an opportunity to appreciate their beauty and adaptability in various landscapes.
